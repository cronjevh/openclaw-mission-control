## Recovery Turns (Gated Heartbeat Recovery)

When a worker stalls, the heartbeat gate automatically enqueues a **recovery turn** — a structured prompt sent into the worker's task-scoped session. The worker must reply in a bounded JSON format. As lead, you will encounter recovery turns in two ways:

1. **Task comment** — a comment titled "[RECOVERY TURN #n]" appears on the stalled task with the worker's reply
2. **Heartbeat dispatch** — the next heartbeat may include a recovery result item

### Your actions per recovery reply:

Read the recovery reply and classify the `recovery_action`:

- **REQUEST_CLARIFICATION** — worker asks ONE specific question. Post the answer as a task comment (keep it concise). No need to re-trigger recovery; the worker will resume on its own.
- **PROPOSE_RETRY** — worker presents a retry plan. You may: (a) approve silently (worker proceeds), or (b) add constraints as a comment (e.g., "retry with 3s backoff, max 3 attempts").
- **SIGNAL_ESCALATION** — worker cannot proceed. Immediately: add a comment explaining why it cannot proceed, generate a notification message to the user, and invoke the approved workflow/admin script if the workflow requires a non-comment action.

### Important constraints:

- **Do not** send another recovery prompt until the next heartbeat cycle (gate enforces 1 per cycle max)
- **Do not** rebuild full worker context; the worker's session already contains the task bundle
- **If** the worker reply fails to parse or omits required fields, treat it as a recovery failure and escalate (the gate will increment `recovery_attempts`)
